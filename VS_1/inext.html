Adding Three Digit Numbers

Step 1: Setting up the problem Stack one number on top of the other number. Make sure to align the ones, tens, and hundreds column.

Step 2: Adding the ones column Add the digits in the ones column. If the number is 10 or higher, write the ones under the line and in the ones column, and write the remaining tens above the tens column.

Step 3: Adding the tens column Add the digits in the tens column. If the number is 10 or highter, write the ones under the line and in the tens column, and write the remaining tens above the hundreds column.

Step 4: Adding the hundreds column Add the digits in the hundreds column. write the number under the hundreds column.

Subtracting three digit numbers

Step 1: Setting up the problemStack one number on top of the other number. Make sure to align the ones, tens, and hundreds column.

Step 2: Subtract the ones column Subtract the digits in the ones column. If the number is less than 0, borrow from the tens column and add 10 to the number in the ones column. Subtract and write the remaining number under the line in the ones column.

Step 3: Subtract the tens columnSubtract the digits in the tens column. If the number is less than 0, borrow from the hundreds column and add 10 to the number in the ones column. Subtract and write the remaining number under the line in the tens column.

Step 4: Subtract the hundreds columnSubtract the digits in the hundreds column. Subtract and write the remaining number under the line in the ones column.

Multiplication

Parts of a Multiplication Problem

There are five parts of a multiplication problem. The first is the multiplicand. This is the number that you are multiplying.

The second part of a multiplication problem is the mutltiplication sign. This tells that you are going to multiply two numbers together

The third part is the multiplier. This can be thought of as how many times you add the multiplicand to itself

The fourth part of a multiplication problem is the equals sign. You should already be familar with this and it signifies the end of the problem

The final part of a multiplication problem is the product. This is the answer to the problem

Let's Solve These Two Problems:

We can think of our multiplicand as a group. In the first problem we have a group of 4

Now we can think of our multiplier as how many groups we have, so we have 2 groups

Finally, we need to find our product.Simply add all of the circles in each group to get our Product which is 8.

Let's solve our second problem:

Again, we can think of our multiplicand as a group. Here we have a group of 3

Remember, our multiplier is as how many groups we have, so this time we have 3 groups

Now add all of our circles together, and our Product is 9.

Division

There are three parts to a division problem. The first part is the Dividend. This is the number being divided

The second part is the Divisor. This is how many groups you're dividing into.

The third and final part of a division problem is the Quotient

Step 1: Our first step is to determine how many times our Divisor can go into the first digit of our Dividend. If our divisor is greater than the first digit than include the second digit. Write this above the last digit that you included.

Step 2: Multiply the first digit in the Quotient by the Divisor and write it below the Dividend.

Step 3: Subtract the number underneath the Quotient and bring down the next digit in the Dividend.

Step 4: If the result is zero then the problem is complete. Otherwise, repeat Steps 1-3 until the Dividend is zero
